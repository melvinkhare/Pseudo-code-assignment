RECYCLING: THE EMANATION

Recycling is a key practice in protecting our environment and conserving natural resources.

By reusing materials like paper, plastic, glass, and metals, we reduce the need for raw material extraction, which saves energy and minimizes environmental damage.

Recycling also helps reduce the volume of waste in landfills, cutting down on pollution and greenhouse gas emissions.

It also helps with the following;
  • Conservation of Natural Resources: Recycling helps preserve natural resources like timber, water, and minerals by reusing materials instead of extracting new raw resources.

  • Energy Savings: Manufacturing products from recycled materials requires less energy than producing them from new materials. For example, recycling aluminum saves up to 95% of the energy needed for creating new aluminum from ore.

  • Environmental Protection: Recycling helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions, which mitigates climate change, and lowers the demand for environmentally harmful processes like mining, deforestation, and oil extraction.
